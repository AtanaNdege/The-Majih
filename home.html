5 Founding Laws

1. Sacred Safety No one is attacked or shamed. This space is zero-tolerance.
2. Listening First Acknowledge the pain before giving advice. Presence matters more than solutions.
3. Honest Anonymity Identity is your choice, never forced. Everyone starts masked.
4. Progress Stories are not meant to stay stuck. Gentle forward movement is encouraged.
5. Purpose Those who heal are quietly invited to support others still hurting.

The Majih Policy

1. About The Majih The Majih is committed to providing compassionate listening, meaningful support and a pathway to healing for individuals in need.
2. Mission To hear without judgement, help with compassion and heal with purpose.
3. Core Values Compassion, respect, confidentiality, integrity, safety and accountability.
4. Code of Conduct Treat all individuals with respect and non-judgment at all times.
5. Protection of Vulnerable Persons We prioritise the protection of vulnerable adults, youth, minors and individuals in crisis.
6. Confidentiality & Privacy Policy All personal information shared with The Majih is treated as strictly confidential.
7. Ethical Listening & Support Policy We must: listen without interrupting or judging, empower individuals rather than create dependency, and encourage professional referrals when necessary.
8. Inclusivity Policy The Majih serves individuals regardless of race, gender, religion, ethnicity, disability, sexual orientation, socio-economic status or immigration status. All are welcome.
9. Data Protection Policy All digital records must be password protected. Personal data will not be sold or shared. Data retention periods must follow applicable laws.
